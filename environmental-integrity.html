TA-14 Applied Domain

Environmental Integrity Governance

Environmental Integrity Governance applies TA-14 admissible-execution discipline to atmospheric evidence, IAQ, HVACDR, refrigeration, sensing, diagnosis, intervention, and post-intervention outcome proof.

Core distinction

Sensors do not replace governance. Sensors may participate in a governed atmospheric evidence chain.

Environmental evidence chain

Environmental Integrity Governance treats sensing and diagnostics as part of a preserved evidence pathway, not merely dashboards or optimization signals.

Observation -> Chronology / Baseline -> Admissibility / Thresholds -> Diagnostic Determination -> Intervention Authority -> Action -> Verified Outcome

Evidence before intervention

Environmental and refrigeration interventions can themselves create consequence-bearing events. TA-14 therefore asks whether sufficient non-invasive evidence exists before physical intervention, refrigerant-side entry, diagnostic contact, or corrective action occurs.

In refrigerant systems, even gauge attachment can create a refrigerant-transfer event through hose inventory and disconnect loss. This makes evidence-before-intervention a governance issue, not merely a procedural preference.

Review focus

  • Atmospheric evidence preservation
  • Baseline and chronology integrity
  • Threshold and diagnostic sufficiency
  • Authority for intervention
  • Non-invasive evidence before consequence-bearing contact
  • Post-intervention outcome proof
  • Continuity from observation through verified result

Applied principle

Environmental reliance should be based on governed atmospheric evidence, not ordinary monitoring alone.
