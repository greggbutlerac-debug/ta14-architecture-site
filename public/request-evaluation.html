Evidence Checklist

What TA-14 looks for during review

Reality

What real-world condition, source state, event, or operating fact is being relied on?

Record

What record proves the condition existed before action or reliance?

Continuity

Can the evidence remain connected across time, tools, actors, systems, and handoffs?

Admissibility

Is the evidence sufficient, current, authorized, and usable before consequence forms?

Binding

Where does the system convert evidence into a claim, decision, reliance, or permission?

Commit

Where does the system become committed to a course of action or irreversible path?

Execution

What action, automation, intervention, publication, transaction, or operational effect occurs?

Outcome

What changed after execution, and how is that outcome recorded and accountable?

Review boundary

A TA-14 review is an architecture-facing admissible-execution review. It is not a legal opinion, security audit, financial audit, regulatory certification, production validation, endorsement, or guarantee.

Public-material reviews are limited to publicly available or submitted materials. A visible gap does not prove private capability is absent unless private capability is reviewed within a defined evidence scope.
