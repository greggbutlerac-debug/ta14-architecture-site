Send enough information to classify the review route.

TA-14 does not need unlimited background at intake. It needs enough information to identify the consequence-bearing route, the evidence being relied on, and the point where reliance, binding, commit, execution, or outcome may occur.

TA-14 chain

Review is mapped against the admissible-execution chain.

TA-14 review looks for evidence, continuity, authority, admissibility, binding discipline, commit control, execution governance, and outcome accountability.

Reality → Record → Continuity → Admissibility → Binding → Commit → Execution → Outcome

Before consequence

TA-14 asks whether the evidence was sufficient, current, authorized, preserved, and scope-bound before reliance, binding, commit, or execution.

After execution

TA-14 asks whether the outcome is recorded, accountable, reviewable, and connected back to the route that allowed consequence to occur.

Review boundary

A TA-14 request is an intake step, not a certification.

A TA-14 review is an architecture-facing admissible-execution review. It is not a legal opinion, security audit, financial audit, regulatory certification, production validation, endorsement, or guarantee.

Public-material reviews are limited to public or submitted materials. A visible gap does not prove private capability is absent unless private capability is reviewed within a defined evidence scope.
