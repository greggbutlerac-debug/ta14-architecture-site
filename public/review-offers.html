TA-14 Admissible Execution Architecture

TA-14 Review Offers

TA-14 reviews help teams, builders, partners, and institutions determine whether a consequence-bearing system has enough governed evidence, continuity, authority, scope, binding discipline, commit control, execution governance, and outcome accountability before reliance or action occurs.

Core review question

Should this system be allowed to bind, commit, execute, or create reliance based on the evidence available right now?

Primary review offers

Offer 01

Public-Material Architecture Review

For organizations, projects, partner systems, or adjacent governance architectures where TA-14 reviews publicly available materials and maps observable strengths, gaps, and admissible-execution posture.

This review is appropriate when a system has public documentation, repositories, white papers, websites, demos, articles, or other externally visible materials that can be mapped against the TA-14 chain.

Review focus:

  • Publicly observable strengths
  • Evidence gaps and missing proof surfaces
  • TA-14 chain mapping
  • Architecture posture and review classification
  • Clear distinction between public evidence and private capability claims

Offer 02

Evidence Gap Review

For teams that need to understand whether their records, logs, sensor data, model outputs, source documents, baselines, thresholds, determinations, or outcome records are strong enough to support reliance.

This review is appropriate when execution, intervention, institutional decision-making, environmental reliance, or AI-assisted action depends on whether the underlying evidence can be trusted.

Review focus:

  • Evidence sufficiency
  • Evidence continuity and chronology
  • Authority and scope separation
  • Reliance conditions
  • Evidence needed before binding, commit, or execution

Offer 03

Production-Readiness Review

For consequence-bearing workflows that need route-complete evidence from reality through outcome before deployment, scaling, certification-readiness, institutional reliance, partner use, or operational execution.

This review is appropriate when a system is moving beyond concept, prototype, public claim, or internal process and must show that no consequence-bearing route can occur outside the admissibility chain.

Review focus:

  • Route-complete admissibility
  • Binding and commit boundary proof
  • Execution control and refusal conditions
  • Outcome accountability
  • Production-readiness gaps and next evidence requirements

Review boundaries

TA-14 reviews are architecture-facing, evidence-first, and strengths-plus-gaps oriented. They do not begin from accusation, endorsement, or assumption of failure.

Unless expressly stated in a written scope, a TA-14 review is not a legal opinion, security audit, regulatory certification, production validation, investment recommendation, or guarantee of system safety.

TA-14 reviews identify what is publicly or operationally evidenced, what remains incomplete, what consequence pathways require stronger proof, and what would be needed to support admissible execution.

Core principle

No admissible evidence. No admissible execution.
