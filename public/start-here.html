TA-14 Admissible Execution Architecture

Start Here: Why TA-14 Exists

Modern systems can generate outputs, recommendations, actions, environmental changes, institutional decisions, and downstream reliance faster than people can verify whether the underlying evidence is still valid.

TA-14 asks one question before consequence occurs:

Should this system be allowed to act, bind, commit, or create reliance based on the evidence available right now?

Why ordinary governance is not enough

Many systems are monitored. Many systems produce logs. Many systems have policies, dashboards, human approvals, or after-the-fact audit trails. Those controls may be useful, but they do not automatically prove that a consequence-bearing action should have been allowed to proceed.

TA-14 focuses on the admissibility of execution itself. It asks whether the chain that produced a proposed action has enough governed evidence, continuity, authority, scope, binding basis, commit discipline, execution control, and outcome accountability before consequence attaches.

The TA-14 chain

TA-14 governs the full admissible-execution stream:

Reality → Record → Continuity → Admissibility → Binding → Commit → Execution → Outcome

This means TA-14 is not merely upstream of execution and not merely downstream after execution. It governs the entire chain through which evidence becomes reliance, reliance becomes binding, binding becomes commit, commit becomes execution, and execution produces an outcome.

What TA-14 protects against

Execution without admissible evidence

A system may act on data, signals, model outputs, sensor readings, or records that have not been sufficiently governed to support consequence-bearing reliance.

Continuity breaks

Evidence may degrade, drift, become stale, lose chronology, or become disconnected from the real-world condition it is supposed to represent.

Authority and scope mismatch

A system may have technical ability to act without proving that the action is authorized, bounded, current, and appropriate for the specific consequence involved.

After-the-fact accountability replacing before-the-fact admissibility

Logs and audits may explain what happened after the fact, but TA-14 asks whether the action should have been allowed before the consequence occurred.

Core TA-14 principle

No admissible evidence. No admissible execution.
