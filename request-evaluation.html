TA-14 Admissible Execution Architecture

Request Evaluation

Use this page to frame a potential TA-14 review. TA-14 evaluations focus on whether a system, workflow, record, evidence process, or partner architecture has enough admissible evidence, continuity, authority, scope, binding discipline, commit control, execution governance, and outcome accountability before consequence-bearing reliance occurs.

Core evaluation question

Should this system be allowed to bind, commit, execute, or create reliance based on the evidence available right now?

Evaluation intake fields

The following fields can be used in an email, form, or intake document to request a TA-14 review. They are designed to clarify the consequence-bearing pathway before review begins.

System / workflow to be reviewed

Identify the system, process, AI workflow, environmental evidence process, institutional decision pathway, partner architecture, or operational procedure being submitted for review.

Consequence-bearing action involved

Describe what action, recommendation, binding step, commit event, intervention, institutional decision, or environmental reliance may occur if the system is trusted.

Who or what relies on the system output?

Identify the users, operators, institutions, downstream systems, clients, occupants, patients, students, customers, regulators, or environments that may rely on the output or action.

What evidence exists before action?

List the records, logs, sensor data, model outputs, human attestations, source documents, observations, baselines, thresholds, diagnostic determinations, or other evidence supporting the action.

What authority allows the action?

Identify who or what has authority to act, approve, bind, commit, intervene, escalate, refuse, or create reliance. Separate technical ability from admissible authority.

Where does binding or commit occur?

Identify the moment where information becomes reliance, reliance becomes obligation, obligation becomes commit, or commit becomes execution.

What happens if the system is wrong?

Describe the operational, financial, environmental, institutional, legal, safety, reputational, or human consequence if the system produces an invalid output or executes under insufficient evidence.

Requested review type

Public-Material Architecture Review

A review based on public materials, documentation, website content, white papers, repositories, papers, demos, or other externally observable evidence.

Evidence Gap Review

A review of whether available records, logs, sensor data, source documents, baselines, thresholds, determinations, and outcome records are sufficient to support admissible reliance.

Partner Alignment Review

A review for adjacent architectures, tools, or governance frameworks seeking to map their role against the TA-14 admissible-execution chain while preserving architectural independence.

Environmental Integrity Review

A review of atmospheric, IAQ, HVACDR, refrigeration, sensor, diagnostic, intervention, or post-intervention evidence pathways where environmental reliance depends on governed records rather than ordinary monitoring alone.

Production-Readiness Review

A deeper review for consequence-bearing workflows that need route-complete evidence from reality through outcome before deployment, scaling, certification-readiness, institutional reliance, or operational execution.

Boundary of review

A TA-14 evaluation is an architecture and admissible-execution review. Unless expressly stated in a written scope, it is not a legal opinion, security audit, compliance certification, endorsement, production validation, or guarantee of system safety.

TA-14 reviews identify strengths, gaps, architectural posture, evidence requirements, admissibility conditions, and possible readiness pathways. Findings are based on the materials and evidence made available for review.

Submission principle

The stronger the evidence package, the stronger the admissibility review can be.
