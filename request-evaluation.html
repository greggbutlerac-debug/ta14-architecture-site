What to Include

Helpful information for an initial request.

1. System or claim What system, workflow, environmental condition, AI output, claim, or decision needs review?
2. Consequence What could happen if the system is allowed to act, bind, commit, publish, intervene, or execute?
3. Evidence available What records, logs, measurements, screenshots, repositories, public pages, or documents currently support the claim?
4. Review need Are you requesting gate review, evidence review, environmental review, architecture review, positioning review, or partner review?
5. Timing and scope Is this exploratory, public-material only, pilot-stage, partner-stage, urgent, or connected to a specific decision?

Boundary

Review does not equal certification.

Submitting a request does not create certification, endorsement, legal opinion, regulatory approval, engineering approval, safety approval, medical judgment, or authorization to execute.

TA-14 review identifies evidence strength, admissibility gaps, boundary conditions, readiness posture, and possible next-step evidence requirements. Any claim of TA-14 alignment, certification, endorsement, or execution readiness requires written TA-14 review and approved language.
