Core evaluation question

Should this system be allowed to bind, commit, execute, or create reliance based on the evidence available right now?

What TA-14 reviews look for

TA-14 reviews focus on whether a system has enough admissible evidence, continuity, authority, scope, binding discipline, commit control, execution governance, and outcome accountability before consequence-bearing reliance occurs.

Evidence before action

What records, logs, sensor data, model outputs, source documents, baselines, thresholds, observations, or determinations support the action?

Continuity and chronology

Has the evidence remained connected, current, preserved, and traceable from reality through the proposed action?

Authority and scope

Who or what is authorized to act, bind, commit, intervene, refuse, or create reliance? Is that authority current and bounded?

Binding, commit, execution, and outcome

Where does reliance become binding? Where does binding become commit? What controls execution? What record proves the outcome?

Requested review types

Public-Material Architecture Review

A review based on websites, repositories, documentation, papers, demos, public claims, and other externally observable material.

Evidence Gap Review

A review of whether records, logs, sensor data, baselines, thresholds, determinations, and outcome records are sufficient to support admissible reliance.

Partner Alignment Review

A review for adjacent architectures or governance systems seeking to map their role against TA-14 while preserving their independent architecture.

Environmental Integrity Review

A review of atmospheric, HVACDR, IAQ, refrigeration, sensor, diagnostic, intervention, or post-intervention evidence pathways.

Production-Readiness Review

A deeper review for consequence-bearing workflows that need route-complete evidence from reality through outcome before deployment, scaling, certification-readiness, or institutional reliance.

Review boundary

A TA-14 review is an architecture and admissible-execution review. Unless expressly stated in a written scope, it is not a legal opinion, security audit, compliance certification, endorsement, production validation, or guarantee of system safety.

TA-14 reviews identify strengths, gaps, architectural posture, evidence requirements, admissibility conditions, and possible readiness pathways based on the materials and evidence made available for review.

Submission principle

The stronger the evidence package, the stronger the admissibility review can be.
