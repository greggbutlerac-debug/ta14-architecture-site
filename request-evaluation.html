Core Review Question

Should this system be allowed to act?

Not just whether it can act. Not just whether it logs what happened. TA-14 asks whether the chain behind action is admissible enough to allow consequence.

Reality → Record → Continuity → Admissibility → Binding → Commit → Execution → Outcome

What to submit

A useful request explains the consequence-bearing route.

TA-14 does not need a perfect packet to start. But the request should make clear what system is being reviewed, what action may occur, what evidence supports that action, who relies on it, where authority comes from, and where binding or commit happens.

A

System or workflow

What system, process, product, architecture, route, or decision pathway should be reviewed?

B

Consequence

What can happen if the system acts, recommends, routes, refuses, escalates, intervenes, or binds?

C

Evidence

What records, documents, logs, sensor data, outputs, diagnostics, or determinations exist before action?

D

Reliance

Who or what relies on the system output, review result, record, recommendation, or decision?

Helpful materials

  • Website, documentation, repository, whitepaper, deck, demo, article, public claim, or diagram.
  • Evidence records, sample logs, source documents, sensor records, diagnostic outputs, or screenshots.
  • Workflow map showing evidence, authority, reliance, binding, commit, execution, refusal, escalation, and outcome.
  • Known limitations, failure modes, uncertainty conditions, review concerns, or specific questions.

Evidence checklist

What TA-14 looks for during review

Reality

What real-world condition, obligation, environment, event, or operational fact is being represented?

Record

What document, log, sensor reading, diagnostic, model output, or determination captures that reality?

Continuity

Can the connection between reality and record be preserved, traced, reviewed, and defended?

Admissibility

Is the evidence strong enough for the level of reliance or consequence involved?

Binding

Where does evidence become reliance, reliance become obligation, or decision constrain action?

Commit

Where does the system enter a hard-to-reverse consequence-bearing route?

Execution

What action, refusal, escalation, intervention, release, routing, or transaction may occur?

Outcome

What record proves what happened after execution and what new reality exists?

Failure mode

What happens if evidence is stale, authority changes, continuity breaks, or the system is wrong?

Review boundaries

Clear boundaries protect the review and the client.

TA-14 reviews are architecture and admissible-execution reviews. They identify strengths, gaps, evidence requirements, route-complete readiness issues, partner boundaries, and review posture.

Not a legal opinion

TA-14 may identify evidence, authority, reliance, continuity, and consequence issues, but it does not provide legal conclusions.

Not a security audit

TA-14 review is not a penetration test, code audit, vulnerability assessment, or cybersecurity certification.

Not certification by default

Review, alignment, readiness, certification-readiness, certification, endorsement, and validation are separate claim categories.

No unsupported private-capability claims

Public-material reviews evaluate visible materials only. A public-material gap means the evidence was not visible in the reviewed public record. It does not prove private capability is absent unless that was specifically reviewed.

Review posture

TA-14 uses evidence-first, chronology-first, continuity-first, strengths-plus-gaps language. Serious adjacent systems are treated seriously. The goal is admissible-execution clarity, not accusation.
