The category

Certification readiness for consequence-bearing systems.

The market already understands compliance, security, AI management systems, risk frameworks, and conformity assessment. TA-14 adds a sharper question: can the system prove why a specific consequence-bearing route was allowed to proceed before people, customers, buildings, institutions, or downstream systems had to rely on the outcome?

1

Evidence before status

TA-14 readiness does not begin with a logo. It begins with records, continuity, authority, scope, reliance, binding, commit control, execution proof, and outcome accountability.

2

Route before reputation

A company may have strong policies and still fail at the point where consequence attaches. TA-14 reviews the route, not just the promise that governance exists somewhere in the organization.

3

Written status before public claims

No company, platform, partner, or governance system may claim TA-14 certification, alignment, approval, backing, or readiness status unless the specific language is approved in writing.

Responsible certification posture

Real certification is not a marketing shortcut. It is a governed status system.

Responsible certification programs require defined criteria, a review scheme, competence, consistency, impartiality, records, scope boundaries, controlled status language, and a way to maintain, suspend, withdraw, or revise status when evidence changes. TA-14 begins with Certification Readiness because the credibility of the category depends on not overclaiming before the proof chain can support it.

01

Defined criteria

Applicants are reviewed against explicit TA-14 admissible-execution criteria, not relationship-based approval.

02

Scoped review object

Status applies only to the reviewed system, route, version, materials, workflow, or evidence object.

03

Preserved record

The finding distinguishes what was reviewed, what was proven, what was incomplete, and what changed.

04

Status governance

Any future TA-14 certification language must be controlled, versioned, time-bounded, and reviewable.

What TA-14 reviews

TA-14 reviews the admissible-execution route that ordinary trust programs often leave implied.

Many governance programs focus on policies, controls, risk registers, management systems, audit trails, security posture, model documentation, or compliance mapping. Those layers matter. TA-14 adds the route question: when a real-world condition becomes a record, and that record becomes reliance, and reliance becomes binding, and binding becomes commit, was execution admissible before consequence attached?

This is why TA-14 belongs beside familiar governance programs. It does not replace legal, compliance, SOC 2, ISO, cybersecurity, financial, or regulatory assessment. It reviews the consequence-bearing chain those programs may not fully adjudicate.

Evidence requirements

Readiness begins with evidence, not aspiration.

TA-14 can begin with public materials, but stronger readiness status requires stronger evidence. The more serious the claim, the more complete the route must be.

1. System description What product, workflow, platform, route, evidence process, or governance architecture is being reviewed?
2. Consequence definition What action, reliance, decision, intervention, refusal, escalation, release, or outcome can occur?
3. Evidence basis What records, logs, documents, sensor data, tests, determinations, claims, or control proofs support the route?
4. Authority and scope Who or what may authorize action, and what limits prevent action outside the reviewed boundary?
5. Binding and commit Where does information become reliance, and where does the system commit to consequence-bearing execution?
6. Outcome accountability What record remains after execution so the result can be reviewed, challenged, corrected, or relied upon?

Review process

From inquiry to readiness finding.

TA-14 Certification Readiness is not an open-ended consulting conversation. The process protects both sides: the applicant gets a credible review path, and TA-14 preserves scope, evidence boundaries, status language, and review integrity.

01

Intake

The applicant submits the system, route, claim, consequence, evidence materials, and intended readiness or status use.

02

Classification

TA-14 classifies the review lane: public-material, evidence-gap, route-complete, partner-review, environmental, or production-readiness.

03

Scope

The exact reviewed object, route, materials, version, boundary, non-claims, and approved use language are defined in writing.

04

Evidence review

TA-14 maps submitted materials against the admissible-execution chain and identifies strengths, gaps, limits, and missing proof.

05

Finding

The review produces a written finding: not reviewable, reviewed, evidence-gap identified, candidate, route-complete reviewed, or production-readiness reviewed.

06

Language control

Any public use of TA-14 language must match the written finding. No self-certification, invented badge, or implied approval is allowed.

07

Registry option

When appropriate, TA-14 may maintain or publish a bounded status entry showing the reviewed scope, status, date, and limits.

08

Renewal / change review

Major system, route, evidence, or public-use changes may require renewed review before status language continues.

Approved language and non-claims

TA-14 status must be precise, scoped, and written.

Certification-readiness value depends on preventing loose claims. TA-14 protects companies, partners, customers, and the public by separating what was actually reviewed from what has not been proven.

MAY SAY

Examples that may be approved after review

  • “Reviewed by TA-14 for admissible-execution evidence gaps.”
  • “TA-14 public-material review completed for the submitted materials dated [date].”
  • “TA-14 evidence-gap finding issued for the reviewed route.”
  • “TA-14 certification-readiness candidate for the scoped route described in the written finding.”
  • “TA-14 route-complete review completed for one defined consequence-bearing route.”
MAY NOT SAY

Language that is not allowed without written authorization

  • “TA-14 certified”
  • “TA-14 approved”
  • “TA-14 endorsed”
  • “TA-14 backed”
  • “TA-14 aligned” as a general unscoped claim
  • “TA-14 partner” without a signed written partner-review framework
  • Any badge, seal, certification mark, or status label not issued or approved by TA-14 in writing
Status follows evidence. Language follows status. Public claims follow written approval.
