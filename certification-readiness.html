Why this category matters

The market is flooded with trust claims. TA-14 asks for the proof route behind consequence.

A company can have policies, controls, dashboards, audit logs, model cards, risk registers, and impressive governance language while still leaving the most important question unresolved: did the actual route from real-world condition to outcome have enough admissible evidence before consequence was allowed to attach?

Certification Readiness is the pathway for organizations that want to stand apart from governance theater and show that their claims are bounded, reviewable, evidence-first, chronology-first, continuity-first, and controlled by written status language.

Evidence before status

Status is not earned by interest, relationship, or branding. It follows the materials, route, scope, and proof reviewed.

02

Route before badge

TA-14 does not reward generalized trust language. It asks what specific consequence-bearing route can survive review.

03

Scope before claim

The reviewed object, version, workflow, evidence packet, and non-claims must be clear before public use.

04

Language before market use

Readiness pathway

A serious status ladder before any public certification claim.

TA-14 uses staged readiness language so serious organizations can participate now without overclaiming. Each status depends on what has actually been reviewed and what the evidence can support.

Not Reviewable

Materials are too incomplete, promotional, vague, contradictory, inaccessible, or unbounded to support a TA-14 finding.

TA-14 Reviewed

Public or submitted materials have entered review. Review occurred; approval has not been implied.

Evidence-Gap Identified

The system shows relevant substance, but specific missing proof prevents readiness or route-complete status.

Certification-Ready Candidate

The system has bounded claims and enough seriousness to proceed toward deeper route-complete review.

Route-Complete Reviewed

One defined consequence-bearing route has been reviewed across the full TA-14 chain.

Production Readiness Reviewed

Higher-level review for organizations preparing to make client-facing, enterprise, partner, or high-consequence use claims.

No reviewed scope. No approved status. No approved language.

Evidence vault

Readiness begins with the record, not the aspiration.

Applicants should be prepared to show what the system does, what consequence may occur, what evidence supports the route, who relies on that evidence, what authority controls action, where commit occurs, and what outcome record remains.

TA-14 can begin with public materials, but stronger status requires stronger evidence. The more serious the claim, the more complete the route must be.

Review process

From inquiry to controlled readiness finding.

TA-14 Certification Readiness is not an open-ended consulting conversation. The process protects the applicant, the market, and TA-14 by preserving scope, evidence boundaries, status language, review integrity, and non-claims.

01

Intake

The applicant submits the system, route, claim, consequence, evidence materials, and intended readiness or status use.

02

Classification

TA-14 classifies the review lane: public-material, evidence-gap, route-complete, partner-review, environmental, or production-readiness.

03

Written scope

The reviewed object, materials, version, boundary, non-claims, and approved use language are defined before deeper value is provided.

04

Evidence review

Submitted materials are mapped against Reality, Record, Continuity, Admissibility, Binding, Commit, Execution, and Outcome.

05

Finding

TA-14 issues a written finding such as not reviewable, reviewed, evidence-gap identified, candidate, route-complete reviewed, or production-readiness reviewed.

06

Language control

Public use of TA-14 language must match the written finding. No self-certification, invented badge, or implied approval is allowed.

07

Registry option

When appropriate, TA-14 may maintain a bounded status entry showing reviewed scope, status, date, and limits.

08

Renewal review

Major system, claim, route, evidence, or public-use changes may require renewed review before status language continues.

Approved language

The value of TA-14 status depends on preventing loose claims.

Certification-readiness language must be precise, scoped, and written. TA-14 separates what was reviewed from what has not been proven.

May say after approval

Scoped, review-backed language

  • “Reviewed by TA-14 for admissible-execution evidence gaps.”
  • “TA-14 public-material review completed for the submitted materials dated [date].”
  • “TA-14 evidence-gap finding issued for the reviewed route.”
  • “TA-14 certification-readiness candidate for the scoped route described in the written finding.”
  • “TA-14 route-complete review completed for one defined consequence-bearing route.”
May not say

Unscoped or unauthorized claims

  • “TA-14 certified” without a formal written status issuance.
  • “TA-14 approved,” “TA-14 endorsed,” or “TA-14 backed.”
  • “TA-14 aligned” as a general unscoped claim.
  • “TA-14 partner” without a signed partner-review framework.
  • Any badge, seal, certification mark, or status label not issued or approved by TA-14 in writing.
Status follows evidence. Language follows status. Public claims follow written approval.

TA-14 sits beside established governance, risk, and conformity programs — and asks a different question.

Organizations may already pursue AI management systems, AI risk management, EU AI Act readiness, SOC 2, cybersecurity review, legal review, quality management, or sector-specific compliance. TA-14 does not replace those systems. It reviews whether the consequence-bearing route can prove admissible execution from reality through outcome.
