Core Doctrine

No admissible evidence.
No admissible execution.

TA-14 governs the full route from real-world condition to outcome accountability.

The TA-14 chain

Execution is not admissible until the route survives the chain.

TA-14 does not ask only whether a system can act. It asks whether reality became record, record remained continuous, evidence became admissible, authority became bound, and execution left an accountable outcome.

RealityWhat is true
RecordWhat is captured
ContinuityWhat stayed intact

Why TA-14 exists

Modern systems are moving faster than their evidence.

AI agents act, dashboards trigger decisions, workflows commit, institutions rely on records, and environmental systems generate claims. TA-14 reviews whether the proof chain is strong enough before consequence-bearing action is allowed.

01

Monitoring is not governance

Monitoring may show conditions. TA-14 asks whether those conditions became preserved, continuous, admissible evidence before reliance occurred.

02

Logging is not admissibility

Logs may explain what happened. TA-14 asks whether action should have been allowed based on the evidence available before execution.

03

Speed is not readiness

A system can be automated, fast, and technically functional while still outrunning the proof required to create consequence.

TA-14 began where execution failure was impossible to miss.

In HVACD/R, systems were being altered before baseline reality was preserved. Measurements were taken out of sequence. Evidence was reconstructed after intervention. Yet safety, warranty, refrigerant, repair, and replacement decisions were made daily.

That field discipline became the foundation for TA-14 Admissible Execution Architecture.
