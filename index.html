Dan Storbaek — useful Stage 1 framing. Use-case registries, model ownership, acceptable-use policies, monitoring, auditability, and human oversight are necessary starting points. But they are not the end state of AI governance. The deeper question is not only: Was the model classified? Was someone assigned ownership? Was there monitoring? Was there a human review path? The harder question is: Was the consequence-bearing execution itself admissible before it was allowed to bind, commit, or produce outcome? That requires a full route, not only a governance checklist: Reality → Record → Continuity → Admissibility → Binding → Commit → Execution → Outcome A system can have a registry, an owner, logs, monitoring, and human oversight while still executing from stale evidence, broken continuity, invalid authority, or an inadmissible record. That is the TA-14 distinction. AI governance cannot stop at visibility. It has to prove admissibility before consequence attaches. No admissible evidence. No admissible execution.
