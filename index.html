TA-14 Chain

No admissible evidence. No admissible execution.

TA-14 governs the full admissible-execution stream from real-world condition through record, continuity, admissibility, binding, commit, execution, and outcome.

Reality → Record → Continuity → Admissibility → Binding → Commit → Execution → Outcome

Core Question

Should this system be allowed to act?

Not merely whether it can act. Not merely whether it logged what happened. TA-14 asks whether the chain behind action is admissible enough to allow consequence.

Evidence Authority Binding Outcome

The problem is not only whether a system works.

Modern systems can generate outputs, route decisions, trigger actions, recommend interventions, or create downstream reliance faster than organizations can verify whether the evidence behind action is still valid. TA-14 asks whether the action should be allowed at all.

Monitoring is not governance

Monitoring can show conditions. TA-14 asks whether records are preserved, continuous, thresholded, admissible, authorized, and accountable before reliance occurs.

Logging is not admissibility

An audit trail may explain what happened after the fact. TA-14 asks whether the evidence chain should have allowed consequence before execution.

Speed is not readiness

A system may act quickly and technically correctly while outrunning the evidence needed to govern consequence.

The governing stream

The TA-14 chain

1
Reality The real-world condition, state, event, environment, or obligation the system claims to represent.
2
Record The captured evidence, source document, log, sensor reading, diagnostic, or determination.
3
Continuity The preserved connection between reality and record across time, transfer, review, and use.
4
Admissibility The determination that evidence is strong enough to support consequence-bearing reliance.
5
Binding The point where evidence becomes reliance, reliance becomes obligation, or decision begins to constrain action.
6
Commit The controlled boundary where the system commits to a consequence-bearing route.
7
Execution The action, intervention, release, refusal, escalation, routing, or decision that creates consequence.
8
Outcome The resulting condition, proof, record, accountability object, or new reality created by execution.

Why TA-14 is different

TA-14 is not just a gate, dashboard, monitor, or audit trail.

TA-14 is the parent admissible-execution architecture. It asks whether the whole route that produced a proposed consequence is admissible enough to allow that consequence to happen.

Evidence before reliance

Information must become governed evidence before it can support reliance.

Authority before action

Technical ability to act is not the same as admissible authority to create consequence.

Outcome after execution

The result of action must be recorded, reviewable, and accountable.
