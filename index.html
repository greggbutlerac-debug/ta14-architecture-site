Core Philosophy

No admissible evidence. No admissible execution.

Modern systems can act faster than organizations can verify reality. TA-14 creates the governance layer between information and consequence: a continuous chain of proof that must exist before a system is permitted to bind, commit, or execute.

TA-14 Chain

Reality → Record → Continuity → Admissibility → Binding → Commit → Execution → Outcome

01

Reality

Identify the actual system, environment, event, or transaction state at the moment of relevance.

02

Record

Preserve a reviewable record with provenance, context, and chronology.

03

Continuity

Confirm the evidence chain has not broken, drifted, substituted, or lost context.

04

Admissibility

Determine whether the evidence is sufficient to support consequential reliance.

05

Binding

Connect intended action to identified authority, scope, and accountable decision posture.

06

Commit

Define the authorized consequence boundary before execution becomes difficult to reverse.

07

Execution

Permit only execution that remains inside the admissible chain and approved boundary.

08

Outcome

Verify what happened, preserve the result, and account for consequence after action.

Important Distinction

Monitoring is not governance.

Monitoring can show conditions. Governance determines whether the record is preserved, continuous, thresholded, admissible, authorized, and accountable before environmental or institutional reliance occurs.
