AI

Use Case 01

AI workflows that can influence action before evidence is reviewable.

AI systems increasingly summarize evidence, recommend next steps, route decisions, trigger escalation, produce operational determinations, or influence human action. TA-14 reviews whether those workflows have enough admissible evidence and governance before the output creates reliance or consequence.

AI agents Model-assisted decisions Recommendation workflows Human-in-the-loop review Refusal and escalation
TA-14 asks What evidence supports the AI output, and is that evidence still current, scoped, and continuous?
Risk condition The model output may appear useful while the evidence chain behind it is incomplete, stale, or unreviewable.
Review target Determine whether the workflow can refuse, narrow, pause, escalate, or fail closed before consequence.

Example AI review questions

  • Does the system distinguish output confidence from admissible evidence?
  • Where does an AI recommendation become human reliance?
  • Can the system detect stale evidence, missing authority, or broken continuity?
  • Is there a controlled commit boundary before execution?
  • What record proves the outcome after action?
ENV

Use Case 02

Environmental integrity pathways where monitoring is not enough.

Environmental systems can sense, log, optimize, diagnose, or trigger intervention. TA-14 Environmental Integrity Governance asks whether atmospheric evidence, IAQ records, HVACDR diagnostics, refrigeration evidence, filtration data, and post-intervention proof remain admissible before reliance or intervention occurs.

IAQ HVACDR Atmospheric evidence Refrigeration diagnostics Evidence before intervention
TA-14 asks Can the environmental record support intervention, reliance, diagnosis, or post-action verification?
Risk condition Monitoring, optimization, intervention, and validation may collapse into one loop without independent evidentiary continuity.
Review target Separate operational action from admissible environmental proof and post-intervention outcome records.

Environmental integrity examples

  • IAQ systems that recommend occupancy, filtration, ventilation, or remediation action.
  • HVACDR workflows where refrigerant-side contact should be governed by non-invasive evidence first.
  • Atmospheric evidence records used to support public, client, school, facility, or operational reliance.
  • Post-intervention performance records showing what changed after action.
  • Sensor-driven decisions where source, continuity, calibration, threshold, and outcome proof matter.

Partner architectures that need second-layer admissible-execution review.

Runtime gates, refusal systems, evidence tools, trust layers, policy engines, audit systems, deterministic execution frameworks, and adjacent governance architectures may each handle part of the chain. TA-14 reviews how those systems relate to the full admissible-execution stream.

Runtime gates Refusal layers Evidence tools Trust systems Partner review network

Partner boundary

TA-14 partner-review work is not a merger, endorsement, private self-certification, or permission to use TA-14 alignment language without written scope. Partner systems keep their architecture. TA-14 provides second-layer admissible-execution review where consequence-bearing execution is involved.

Use Case 04

Institutional decisions where reliance can become binding before proof catches up.

Organizations often move from information to reliance to decision to obligation without clearly identifying where the evidence threshold was crossed. TA-14 helps determine whether institutional decision routes are governed before they create consequence.

Decision routes Client reliance Operational approvals Authority and scope Outcome accountability
TA-14 asks Where does information become reliance, and where does reliance become binding?
Risk condition The organization may create obligation before evidence, authority, or outcome accountability is clear.
Review target Identify the binding point, commit boundary, authority chain, and outcome proof record.

Institutional decision examples

  • Client-facing claims based on incomplete or unreviewed evidence.
  • Operational actions based on dashboards, summaries, or recommendations.
  • Partner pathways where one system's output becomes another system's authority input.
  • Administrative decisions that create reliance, obligations, or downstream records.
  • Public or internal decisions that require chronology and evidence preservation.

Runtime pressure scenarios

TA-14 matters most when the system is under pressure.

The real test is not whether governance language exists in calm conditions. The real test is whether the system can remain governable when evidence degrades, timelines compress, dependencies drift, human oversight weakens, or procedural momentum begins carrying execution forward.

Runtime dependency drift

A system continues operating while an upstream source, authority condition, or environmental state changes. TA-14 asks whether enough admissible governable space remains to pause, narrow, reassess, or refuse.

Procedural momentum

A workflow is still formally reviewable, but rollback cost, synchronization dependency, and decision pressure make intervention operationally unrealistic. TA-14 tests whether intervention remains real.

Authority drift

Authority was valid earlier but becomes invalid before binding, commit, or execution. TA-14 asks whether the route can detect that change before consequence attaches.

Evidence outrun

The system moves faster than the evidence can support. TA-14 reviews whether execution has outrun the admissibility capacity of the evidence record.

Ambiguity escalation

The system has incomplete or conflicting evidence. TA-14 asks whether it can narrow, pause, escalate, refuse, or fail closed instead of converting ambiguity into consequence.

Outcome proof failure

Execution happens, but no adequate record proves what changed after action. TA-14 treats outcome accountability as part of the admissible-execution chain.

Is TA-14 a fit?

Use TA-14 when consequence depends on evidence.

If the system only displays information
Ordinary documentation, monitoring, or UX review may be enough.
TA-14 becomes relevant when that information is used to justify action, reliance, obligation, or intervention.
If the system only logs events
Audit logging may preserve what happened.
TA-14 becomes relevant when the log is used as evidence that execution was allowed, justified, or accountable.
If the system recommends action
Recommendation quality, model accuracy, or usability may matter.
TA-14 becomes relevant when people, systems, or institutions rely on the recommendation to act.
If the system can trigger intervention
Operational performance and control logic matter.
TA-14 becomes relevant when intervention creates consequence and requires evidence before action.
If the system claims governance
Policies, dashboards, controls, and audit trails may support governance.
TA-14 becomes relevant when the governance claim depends on route-complete admissible execution.

Simple fit test

If a system can make something happen, cause someone to rely, trigger action, bind a decision, support intervention, or create an outcome, TA-14 can review whether that consequence-bearing route is admissible enough to proceed.
