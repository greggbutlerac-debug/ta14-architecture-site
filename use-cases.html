Use Case 01

AI agents and workflows that can act, route, recommend, purchase, approve, refuse, or escalate.

AI systems increasingly move beyond answers into tool use and operational consequence. TA-14 asks whether the evidence, authority, continuity, reliance basis, and commit boundary are admissible before the agent is allowed to matter.

AI agents Tool calls Recommendations Human reliance Refusal and escalation
Gate route POST /v1/evaluate-execution, supported by evidence, authority, and continuity checks.
Likely decision states ALLOW when the route holds; HOLD for missing evidence; DENY for invalid authority; ESCALATE for high-risk ambiguity.
Best service fit API Readiness, Route-Complete Review, or Runtime Readiness depending on maturity.

Evidence required before action

  • Current source records supporting the proposed action.
  • Identity and delegated authority for the actor and action.
  • Continuity between evidence, model output, tool call, and commit point.
  • A defined refusal, hold, or escalation path when uncertainty remains.
  • An outcome record showing what happened after execution.

Environmental evidence requirements

Pressure scenarios

The architecture matters most when ordinary controls begin to drift.

Evidence outrun

The system moves faster than the evidence can support. The correct result is often HOLD, not forced confidence.

Authority drift

Authority was once valid but expires, changes, or becomes out of scope before commit. The route should deny or revalidate.

Continuity break

The source, sequence, timing, lineage, or custody no longer holds. Reliance should stop until continuity is restored.

Ambiguity escalation

Conflicting or incomplete evidence should become ESCALATE, not an unsupported allow or deny.

Procedural momentum

The route remains formally reviewable but operational pressure makes reversal unrealistic. TA-14 tests whether the stop is still real.

Outcome memory failure

Execution occurs, but no sufficient record proves what changed or what the next decision should inherit.
