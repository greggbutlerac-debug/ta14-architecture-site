What happens next

1. Reviewability

TA-14 determines whether the submitted materials reveal enough of the system, route, evidence, authority, and consequence to support a defensible review.

2. Scope routing

The matter is routed into Reviewability, API Readiness, Evidence Gap, Route-Complete, Runtime, Procurement, Partner API, or Enterprise scope.

3. Evidence request

If key records or details are missing, TA-14 may request supporting materials before a stronger finding or larger engagement can be defined.

$49 credit-forward rule

When a paid $49 Reviewability Check advances into the $250 API Readiness Check, the $49 is credited toward that next scope.

Send supporting materials when needed

The strongest follow-up package includes the system, consequence-bearing action, evidence source, authority basis, reliance point, binding and commit boundary, failure exposure, desired outcome, budget, and timeline.

  • Website, repository, document, API page, architecture diagram, or public claim
  • What action, refusal, intervention, obligation, or outcome may occur
  • What evidence exists before action
  • Who or what has authority to permit the action
  • Who or what relies on the output or decision
  • Where binding and commit occur
  • What happens if the system is wrong or cannot prove the route
  • What result you want from TA-14
