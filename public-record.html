TA-14 Admissible Execution Architecture

Public Record and Preservation Anchors

TA-14 is supported by public chronology, preservation materials, repositories, publications, and architecture pages. These anchors help preserve provenance, continuity, and public review context.

Purpose of public anchors

Public anchors support chronology, transparency, reviewability, and preservation. They do not automatically certify, endorse, or validate third-party systems.

Environmental Integrity Governance applies TA-14 evidence discipline to atmospheric, HVACDR, sensing, intervention, and post-intervention proof pathways.

TA-14 Academy

TA-14 Academy materials translate admissible-execution concepts into structured training and evidence-before-intervention learning pathways.

Boundary note

Public resources support review and preservation, but public availability is not the same as production validation. A third-party system should not claim TA-14 certification, TA-14 approval, TA-14 endorsement, or TA-14 production-readiness status unless that status has been expressly granted in writing under a defined review scope.

Preservation principle

Chronology matters because admissibility depends on preserved evidence, preserved continuity, and preserved authority over time.
