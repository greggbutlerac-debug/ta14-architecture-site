No Build Before Boundary

Do not build the route before the boundary is written.

TA-14 can provide high-level fit assessment before formal scope. But deeper architecture shaping, partner-review enablement, evidence packet design, route definitions, test matrices, maturity roadmaps, claim-boundary structures, and implementation guidance require written scope, payment, reciprocity, or a defined engagement.

This rule protects TA-14 from being converted into free architecture labor, protects partners from ambiguous claims, and protects clients from confusing review language before a legitimate review boundary exists.

Core principle

No build before boundary.

TA-14 is an admissible-execution review architecture. Its chain, language, evidence standards, partner review pathways, and public credibility have value. They should not be converted into unpaid implementation design for another system before a written boundary exists.

Safe posture

TA-14 may review whether a conversation, architecture, partner lane, or client pathway appears to fit. But if the next step creates reusable architecture, client-facing delivery structure, implementation material, route-complete evidence objects, test standards, or partner-review operating logic, the work must move into written scope.

Allowed before written scope

High-level fit assessment can happen before engagement.

1

Basic orientation

Explaining what TA-14 is, what it is not, and whether a system appears adjacent to admissible-execution review.

2

Initial boundary discussion

Clarifying whether the other system is independent, overlapping, partner-track, referral-only, or review-candidate.

3

Public-material review interest

Determining whether public materials are enough to support a scoped TA-14 architecture or evidence gap review.

4

Non-claim clarification

Stating that no certification, endorsement, merger, partnership, alignment, or TA-14 status exists without writing.

5

Review lane classification

Classifying a possible lane as boundary-only, referral-only, scoped review candidate, partner-track candidate, or strategic partner.

6

Next-step offer

Offering a paid review, reciprocal exchange, partner discussion, or written scope as the next step.

Requires written scope

Anything reusable, operational, or client-facing must be scoped.

Route definitions Mapping another system’s execution routes, authority routes, refusal routes, partner routes, environmental routes, or client delivery pathways.
Test matrices Misexecution tests, pressure tests, failure-mode scenarios, admissibility thresholds, escalation tests, refusal tests, or production-readiness test suites.
Maturity roadmaps Readiness ladders, certification-readiness plans, implementation maturity models, evidence maturity scoring, or route-complete advancement roadmaps.
Partner-review templates Reusable partner offer sheets, client scopes, operating models, revenue structures, approved-language templates, network positioning, or partner enablement materials.
Implementation guidance Technical build direction, workflow design, proof-system design, automation structures, governance operations, or production-deployment guidance.

Hard stop

If the discussion starts producing assets the other party can reuse to build, sell, position, certify, claim readiness, win a client, or operate a governance layer, TA-14 must stop and move to written scope.

Partner-review boundary

Partner-track conversations need the strongest boundary.

Partner-review discussions are valuable because TA-14 can create real credibility, structure, and client value. That is exactly why the boundary must be explicit before TA-14 gives away the operating model.

Before written partner scope

  • Discuss fit.
  • Classify the lane.
  • Clarify non-claims.
  • Identify possible client value.
  • Offer written scope.

After written partner scope

  • Define review lane.
  • Approve language.
  • Set revenue or reciprocity terms.
  • Map evidence requirements.
  • Deliver client-facing review structure.

Why claim control matters

TA-14 status language can influence trust, credibility, market positioning, partner claims, client confidence, and perceived readiness. That language must not be used before the evidence, review scope, and authorization exist.

Why it matters

Without the boundary, the architecture can be extracted without the review.

TA-14’s value is not only in its words. Its value is in the sequence, evidence discipline, route-complete review logic, admissibility chain, decision classes, partner language, and public authority that make the review credible. If those are transferred before scope, another party can receive the architecture value without the accountability, evidence, reciprocity, or TA-14 participation.

Protect clients

Clean rule
