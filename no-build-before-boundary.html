TA-14 Admissible Execution Architecture

No Build Before Boundary

TA-14 supports public education, architecture review, and constructive engagement. But deeper architecture shaping, evidence design, implementation guidance, maturity planning, test matrices, and route definitions require a clear paid or reciprocal boundary.

Boundary rule

Free interaction may identify fit and public-material gaps. Solution shaping begins only after payment, reciprocity, attribution, partnership, or another clear value exchange.

What may happen before scope

  • Brief fit assessment
  • Neutral public-material gap identification
  • High-level explanation of TA-14 review posture
  • Short threshold comments on whether a system may be reviewable
  • Referral to a paid review, partner-review pathway, or reciprocal evidence exchange

What requires paid or reciprocal scope

  • Custom evidence packet design
  • Route definitions or execution-path mapping
  • Test matrices or conformance planning
  • Implementation guidance
  • Maturity roadmaps
  • Architecture development for another party
  • Deep conceptual refinement of another system using TA-14 doctrine
  • Certification-readiness positioning or review language

Why this boundary exists

TA-14 review and architecture language can create real value for other systems. If a party receives architecture value, TA-14 should receive payment, evidence, attribution, partnership, or another clear form of reciprocity.

The boundary is not hostile. It preserves professional scope, protects chronology, prevents asymmetric value extraction, and ensures that TA-14 review work remains sustainable.

Professional redirect

This is the point where the conversation should move from open discussion into a defined TA-14 review scope, reciprocal partner framework, written attribution, evidence exchange, or paid engagement.
