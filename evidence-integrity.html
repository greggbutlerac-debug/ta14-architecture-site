Evidence Integrity Governance

Information is not evidence until it is governed.

Evidence Integrity Governance asks what must be true before information can be relied upon as evidence. TA-14 treats evidence as a governed lifecycle, not a loose collection of data, logs, screenshots, or claims.

Governing Question

What must be true of evidence before admissibility can exist?

Evidence must preserve source, context, chronology, continuity, sufficiency, scope, and reviewability before it can support binding action.

Evidence Conditions

Evidence must survive pressure.

Continuity

The chain from observation to record to review must remain intact.

Capacity

The evidence system must be able to support the decision burden placed on it.

Health

Evidence must remain current, interpretable, and suitable for reliance.

Survivability

Evidence must remain governable under runtime pressure, delay, drift, and procedural momentum.
