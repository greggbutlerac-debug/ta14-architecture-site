Pricing boundary

Low-cost review does not mean unlimited architecture development.

Fixed-fee TA-14 review may identify gaps, classify evidence, map review findings, and define what is incomplete. Custom implementation guidance, route design, test matrices, evidence packet design, maturity roadmaps, partner operating models, and production-readiness build direction require separate written scope.

TA-14 keeps the first review accessible so teams can see the admissibility gap early. Deeper architecture shaping begins only after scope, boundary, and value exchange are clear.

Request a TA-14 review.

TA-14 reviews systems, workflows, evidence processes, partner architectures, runtime-gate pathways, environmental integrity routes, and institutional decision routes where consequence-bearing action, reliance, intervention, or outcome may occur.

To request a TA-14 review, include:

  1. What system, workflow, evidence process, or architecture should be reviewed.
  2. What consequence-bearing action, reliance, intervention, or decision may occur.
  3. What evidence currently supports that action.
  4. Who or what relies on the evidence.
  5. Where authority, binding, commit, execution, and outcome records appear.
