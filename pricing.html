What the fixed fee buys

A review finding, not an open-ended consulting engagement.

TA-14 review is designed to show what is strong, what is partial, what is incomplete, and what cannot yet be treated as admissible enough for reliance, binding, commit, execution, or outcome accountability.

1

Architecture-facing finding

TA-14 maps the submitted route against Reality, Record, Continuity, Admissibility, Binding, Commit, Execution, and Outcome.

2

Evidence gap clarity

The review distinguishes what is shown, what is claimed, what is missing, and where reliance would be premature.

3

Bounded next step

The finding can support a paid deeper review, partner lane, or production-readiness scope without giving away unlimited architecture work.

Pricing boundary

Low-cost review does not mean unlimited architecture development.

Fixed-fee TA-14 review may identify gaps, classify evidence, map review findings, and define what is incomplete. Custom implementation guidance, route design, test matrices, evidence packet design, maturity roadmaps, partner operating models, and production-readiness build direction require separate written scope.

TA-14 keeps the first review accessible so teams can see the admissibility gap early. Deeper architecture shaping begins only after scope, boundary, and value exchange are clear.

Request a TA-14 review.

Send the route, the consequence, the evidence, the relying party, and where authority, binding, commit, execution, and outcome records appear. TA-14 will identify the proper review lane before deeper architecture value is provided.

To request a TA-14 review, include:

  1. What system, workflow, evidence process, or architecture should be reviewed.
  2. What consequence-bearing action, reliance, intervention, or decision may occur.
  3. What evidence currently supports that action.
  4. Who or what relies on the evidence.
  5. Where authority, binding, commit, execution, and outcome records appear.
