What partner-review entity means

A partner-review entity means there is a recognized pathway for scoped work where the entity contributes a specialized layer and TA-14 contributes the admissible-execution review layer. It does not mean automatic certification, public endorsement, merger, shared ownership, or permission to self-use TA-14 status language outside approved wording.

How partner review works

Specialized first layer. TA-14 second layer.

1

First-layer specialized review

The partner evaluates the domain it knows best: runtime control, evidence collection, implementation, compliance support, building operations, environmental pathways, review readiness, or specialized operational review.

2

Second-layer TA-14 review

TA-14 reviews whether the consequence-bearing pathway is admissibly governed across the full chain from reality through outcome.

Partner claims remain partner claims. TA-14 findings remain TA-14 findings.

TA-14 role

TA-14 is the parent admissible-execution review layer.

TA-14 is not trying to absorb adjacent systems. It provides the admissible-execution review layer that helps determine whether consequence-bearing action should be allowed at all.

Reality

What condition, event, system state, or environmental fact is being relied upon?

Record

What record preserves that reality, and is the source known?

Continuity

Can the route prove the record was not broken, detached, stale, or contaminated?

Admissibility

Is the evidence strong enough to support reliance?

Binding

Where does reliance attach to a decision, permission, obligation, or route?

Commit

Where does the pathway become difficult to reverse?

Execution

What action, intervention, refusal, escalation, or system behavior occurs?

Outcome

What proof shows what happened after execution?

Shared client value

When TA-14 review authority, language, credibility, or chain mapping helps create client value, TA-14 participates in the engagement through written scope, fee, revenue share, referral agreement, or another reciprocal exchange.

Claim control

TA-14 language cannot be self-used.

Potential approved language

  • TA-14 partner-review pathway
  • TA-14 boundary-reviewed
  • TA-14 admissible-execution gap review
  • TA-14 second-layer review
  • Certification-readiness language, if specifically approved

Restricted language

  • TA-14 certified
  • TA-14 backed
  • TA-14 endorsed
  • TA-14 approved
  • TA-14 aligned
  • TA-14 certification-ready, unless written approval exists

No self-certification

No partner may self-certify as TA-14 reviewed, TA-14 aligned, TA-14 backed, TA-14 approved, or TA-14 certification-ready without written authorization. No partner may privately deliver the TA-14 layer while representing it as TA-14 review.

Boundaries

The network is strong because the boundary is strong.

No merger

Partner-review status does not merge TA-14 with any partner entity or make either architecture the property of the other.

No blanket endorsement

Partnership, review discussion, or client scope does not automatically endorse every claim, tool, system, implementation, or public statement.

No uncontrolled implementation

Deeper architecture shaping, evidence packet design, route definitions, test matrices, maturity roadmaps, or implementation guidance require paid or reciprocal engagement.

No build before boundary

TA-14 may provide high-level fit assessment before written scope. But partner-review templates, operating model, enablement material, architecture-shaping guidance, proof-object design, implementation guidance, and claim-boundary structures require written acceptance and defined scope.
