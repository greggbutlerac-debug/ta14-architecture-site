Why the network exists

Serious adjacent systems should not be erased. They should be reviewed through clear admissible-execution boundaries.

Independent architecture → specialized review → TA-14 admissible-execution review → client value

The strongest governance ecosystem is not one where every serious system is forced into a single name. The strongest ecosystem is one where specialized architectures keep their strengths, clients receive layered review, and TA-14 evaluates whether consequence-bearing execution is admissible across the full chain.

01

Partners keep their identity

A partner’s architecture remains its own architecture. TA-14 does not absorb, rename, merge, or subordinate it merely because a partner-review lane exists.

02

TA-14 reviews consequence

TA-14 asks whether the route is admissible enough before it binds, commits, executes, or creates outcome.

03

Clients get layered proof

A client can receive the partner’s specialized assessment plus TA-14’s second-layer admissible-execution boundary / gap review.

What active partner-review lane means

An active partner-review lane means there is a recognized pathway for scoped work where the partner contributes its specialized review layer and TA-14 contributes the second-layer admissible-execution review. It does not mean automatic certification, public endorsement, merger, shared ownership, or permission to self-use TA-14 status language outside approved wording.

Network model

The Partner Review Network has two main paths.

Bilateral partner-review lane

One partner plus TA-14 define a written lane for client work.

  • Partner performs its specialized assessment.
  • TA-14 performs admissible-execution boundary / gap review.
  • Public language is controlled.
  • Revenue and role are agreed before work begins.
  • No implied certification or endorsement exists.

Multi-partner review-network lane

TA-14 may coordinate more than one specialized partner layer when a client’s risk profile requires it.

  • Each partner keeps its own review layer.
  • Only scoped layers participate.
  • TA-14 reviews the full admissible-execution route.
  • Participation and economics are written in advance.
  • No partner is paid merely for being listed in the network.

Simple rule

Partners are paid and named when their review layer is included in the written client scope. TA-14 is included when TA-14 language, credibility, review authority, alignment language, certification-readiness positioning, or admissible-execution review creates client value.

No self-certification

No partner, adjacent system, client, vendor, reviewer, consultant, or collaborator may self-certify as TA-14-aligned, TA-14-backed, TA-14-reviewed, TA-14-approved, TA-14-certified, or TA-14 partner without written approval from TA-14.

Boundaries

The network is strong because the boundary is strong.

No merger

A partner-review pathway does not merge TA-14 with the partner architecture or make either system a subsystem of the other.

No endorsement by default

Review interest, partner discussion, or scoped review does not automatically endorse the partner, client, tool, system, or implementation.

No certification by default

Partner-review activity does not create certification unless a certification-readiness or certification scope is separately written and approved.

No private TA-14 layer delivery

Partners may not privately deliver the TA-14 layer, TA-14 review status, or TA-14 admissible-execution authority without TA-14 participation and written approval.

No uncontrolled public use

Public use of TA-14 name, chain, review authority, logo, partner framing, or status language requires approved wording.

No build before boundary

Templates, operating model, implementation guidance, evidence-object design, route mapping, and maturity roadmaps require defined scope.

Boundary rule

Do not share partner-review templates, enablement materials, implementation roadmaps, claim-boundary structures, or architecture-shaping guidance before written acceptance and scope. The network exists to create client value, not to give away TA-14’s operating model.
