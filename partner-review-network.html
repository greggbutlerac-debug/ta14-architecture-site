TA-14 Admissible Execution Architecture

TA-14 Partner Review Network

TA-14 can work with serious adjacent governance builders, review specialists, and implementation partners while preserving each architecture's independent role.

Partner principle

Partners keep their system. TA-14 provides the parent admissible-execution review layer across Reality -> Record -> Continuity -> Admissibility -> Binding -> Commit -> Execution -> Outcome.

How partner review works

First-layer specialized review

The partner evaluates the domain they know best, such as runtime control, evidence collection, implementation, compliance support, domain workflow, or specialized operational review.

Second-layer TA-14 review

TA-14 reviews whether the consequence-bearing pathway is admissibly governed across the full chain from reality through outcome.

Separated claims

Partner claims remain partner claims. TA-14 findings remain TA-14 findings. Alignment language, certification-readiness language, or TA-14-backed positioning requires written approval.

Shared client value

When TA-14 review authority, language, credibility, or chain mapping helps create client value, TA-14 should participate in the engagement through a written scope, fee, revenue share, referral agreement, or other reciprocal exchange.

Use restrictions

No partner may self-certify as TA-14 reviewed, TA-14 aligned, TA-14 backed, TA-14 approved, or TA-14 certification-ready without written authorization. No partner may privately deliver the TA-14 layer while representing it as TA-14 review.

TA-14 may provide high-level fit assessment before a written scope, but deeper architecture shaping, evidence packet design, route definitions, test matrices, maturity roadmaps, or implementation guidance require paid or reciprocal engagement.

Network posture

TA-14 is not trying to absorb adjacent systems. It provides the admissible-execution review layer that helps determine whether consequence-bearing action should be allowed at all.
