Environmental Integrity Governance

Air needs evidence, continuity, and accountability.

Environmental Integrity Governance separates ordinary environmental monitoring from governed atmospheric evidence. Sensors can measure air. EIG governs whether those measurements become reliable, preserved, threshold-based, and accountable records.

Core Distinction

Monitoring is not governing indoor air.

Continuous monitoring can show environmental conditions. It does not automatically establish evidence integrity, baseline chronology, threshold legitimacy, intervention authority, diagnostic determination, or post-intervention proof.

Atmospheric Integrity Records

A building can have an atmospheric history.

Outdoor Context

Records exterior environmental pressure and source conditions affecting the building boundary.

Indoor Protection

Records how HVAC, filtration, ventilation, and envelope behavior shape the interior atmosphere.

Threshold Events

Preserves moments where environmental conditions cross relevance, risk, or intervention thresholds.

Outcome Proof

Verifies whether intervention actually improved environmental integrity after action.

Applied Domains

HVAC, filtration, IAQ, buildings, and community visibility.

EIG is especially relevant where communities, buildings, or institutions need more than data points. They need atmospheric evidence systems that can preserve conditions over time, establish thresholds, and support accountability.
