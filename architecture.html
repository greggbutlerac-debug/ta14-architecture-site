Parent Architecture

The full chain from reality to consequence.

TA-14 is not merely upstream or downstream of execution. It governs the entire admissible-execution stream: Reality → Record → Continuity → Admissibility → Binding → Commit → Execution → Outcome.

Governing Question

Can anything consequential happen outside the admissibility chain?

Production-grade admissibility requires consequence-bearing routes to be registered, governed, tested, fail-closed, source-grounded, authority-validated, scope-constrained, bind/commit-receipted, execution-controlled, replayable, and outcome-accounted.

Eight Stages

Proof before action.

RealityWhat is the actual state of the system, environment, person, transaction, or event?
RecordWhat preserved evidence exists, and does it include context, provenance, chronology, and source?
ContinuityDid the evidence chain remain intact from observation through review and decision?
AdmissibilityIs the evidence sufficient to support reliance, escalation, refusal, or action?
BindingWhat authority, scope, and responsibility are connected to the proposed action?
CommitWhat boundary is being crossed before execution becomes consequential or difficult to reverse?
ExecutionDoes the action remain inside the authorized and admissible boundary?
OutcomeWhat happened, and can the result be verified, preserved, and accounted for?
