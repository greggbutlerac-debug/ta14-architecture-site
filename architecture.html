Architecture

TA-14 governs whether execution should be allowed before consequence happens.

TA-14 Admissible Execution Architecture is a full-chain governance architecture for consequence-bearing systems. It does not merely ask whether a system can act, whether an event was logged, or whether a policy was present. It asks whether the evidence, continuity, authority, scope, binding basis, commit boundary, execution control, and outcome record are sufficient to allow action at all.

The problem TA-14 addresses

Modern systems can create reliance, trigger actions, route decisions, recommend interventions, authorize work, change environments, or produce downstream consequences faster than people can verify whether the underlying evidence remains valid. A system may be monitored, logged, audited, policy-aligned, or technically correct and still be operating on evidence that is incomplete, stale, unauthoritative, discontinuous, or insufficient for consequence-bearing reliance.

This is the gap TA-14 addresses. TA-14 is not satisfied by a record that something happened after the fact. It asks whether the chain that allowed the action was admissible before execution occurred.

Core risk

A system can execute correctly at the technical level and still misexecute if the evidence, continuity, authority, scope, binding basis, commit decision, execution control, or outcome accountability is invalid or incomplete.

What TA-14 is

TA-14 is an admissible-execution architecture. It governs the conditions under which consequence-bearing execution may proceed. It is built around one governing question:

Should this system be allowed to bind, commit, execute, or create reliance based on the evidence available right now?

TA-14 treats execution as more than a final action. Execution is the visible end of a deeper evidentiary chain. Before a system acts, something was treated as real. That reality was recorded. The record was preserved or transformed. Continuity was either maintained or degraded. A determination of admissibility was made or assumed. Reliance became binding. Binding moved toward commit. Commit authorized execution. Execution produced an outcome.

TA-14 governs that full progression.

The TA-14 admissible-execution chain

The core TA-14 chain is:

Reality → Record → Continuity → Admissibility → Binding → Commit → Execution → Outcome

1
Reality The real-world condition, event, state, environment, obligation, or operational fact that the system claims to represent or act upon.
2
Record The captured evidence, observation, signal, log, sensor reading, source document, human attestation, diagnostic determination, or data object representing reality.
3
Continuity The preservation of connection between reality and record across time, transfer, transformation, interpretation, review, and use.
4
Admissibility The threshold determination that evidence is sufficient, current, authoritative, scoped, and reliable enough to support consequence-bearing reliance.
5
Binding The point where evidence becomes reliance, reliance becomes obligation, or a determination begins to constrain downstream action.
6
Commit The controlled boundary where a system, person, workflow, agent, institution, or process commits to a consequence-bearing path.
7
Execution The action, intervention, decision, routing, release, refusal, escalation, transaction, or operational movement that produces consequence.
8
Outcome The resulting condition, consequence, record, verification state, accountability object, or new reality created by execution.

Architecture layers

TA-14 is not a single checkpoint. It is an architecture family for governing evidence, reliance, authority, execution, outcome, institutional memory, and review discipline. These layers help explain why TA-14 is broader than monitoring, policy, logging, or ordinary audit.

Parent chain
Reality → Record → Continuity → Admissibility → Binding → Commit → Execution → Outcome. This is the governing spine of TA-14.
Evidence Governance
Governs whether information can become evidence, whether evidence remains healthy, whether it survives pressure, and whether it can support admissibility.
Reliance Governance
Governs what must be true before a person, system, institution, workflow, or downstream process may rely on evidence or determinations.
Authority Governance
Separates technical ability from admissible authority. A system may be able to act without being authorized to create consequence.
Binding and Commit Governance
Identifies the transition from information to reliance, reliance to obligation, obligation to commit, and commit to execution.
Execution Governance
Controls whether action proceeds, narrows, pauses, escalates, refuses, or fails closed before consequence attaches.
Outcome Accountability
Preserves the result of execution, verifies post-intervention state, and records the new reality produced by action.
Institutional Memory
Preserves doctrine, precedent, review findings, lineage, changes, and supersession so TA-14 itself remains governable over time.

TA-14 and misexecution

TA-14 distinguishes ordinary technical failure from misexecution. A system may produce the expected output, follow programmed rules, complete the requested operation, and leave an audit trail while still executing on an inadmissible basis.

Technical failure

The system malfunctioned, crashed, produced an incorrect output, violated a rule, failed a test, or did not perform as designed.

Misexecution

The system acted or created consequence when the evidence, continuity, authority, scope, binding basis, commit decision, execution control, or outcome accountability was not admissible.

The TA-14 question is not only “did the system work?” It is “should the system have been allowed to act?”

Evidence Governance inside TA-14

TA-14 treats evidence as governed infrastructure, not passive information. A data point, model output, sensor reading, document, log, or human statement does not automatically become admissible evidence merely because it exists. Evidence must be classified, preserved, connected, tested, scoped, and evaluated before it can support reliance.

Evidence continuity

Can the evidence still be traced back to the real condition, source, or event it claims to represent?

Evidence health

Is the evidence complete, current, coherent, uncorrupted, and suitable for the consequence involved?

Evidence capacity

Is the evidence strong enough to support the level of reliance, binding, commit, or execution being proposed?

Evidence survivability

Does the evidence remain admissible under runtime pressure, latency, drift, uncertainty, or changing conditions?

Evidence-to-admissibility transition

At what point does evidence become strong enough to support admissible reliance?

Evidence outrun condition

Has the system moved faster than its evidence can safely govern?

What TA-14 is not

Clear boundaries are important because TA-14 can be misread if it is reduced to familiar categories.

TA-14 is not ordinary monitoring

Monitoring observes activity. TA-14 governs whether the evidentiary basis for action is admissible before consequence-bearing execution occurs.

TA-14 is not audit logging

Logs may show what happened. TA-14 asks whether what happened should have been allowed at all.

TA-14 is not a policy dashboard

Dashboards may display controls. TA-14 governs the chain through which evidence becomes action and outcome.

TA-14 is not only an execution gate

TA-14 includes execution-boundary governance, but its scope begins at reality and continues through outcome.

How TA-14 is used in review

TA-14 reviews map systems, workflows, public materials, evidence packages, partner architectures, environmental processes, and institutional decision routes against the admissible-execution chain. The review posture is evidence-first, chronology-first, continuity-first, and strengths-plus-gaps.

Public-material architecture review

Maps public documentation, repositories, sites, claims, demos, and visible design evidence against the TA-14 chain without making unsupported claims about private capability.

Evidence gap review

Identifies missing records, continuity breaks, unclear authority, weak reliance basis, or incomplete outcome evidence before action or institutional reliance.

Production-readiness review

Evaluates whether consequence-bearing routes are governed from live reality through record, continuity, admissibility, binding, commit, execution, and outcome.

Review principle

No admissible evidence. No admissible execution.
