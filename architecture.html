Architecture

TA-14 governs whether execution should be allowed before consequence happens.

TA-14 Admissible Execution Architecture is a full-chain governance architecture for consequence-bearing systems. It asks whether the evidence, continuity, authority, scope, binding basis, commit boundary, execution control, and outcome record are sufficient to allow action at all.

The problem TA-14 addresses

Modern systems can create reliance, trigger actions, route decisions, recommend interventions, authorize work, change environments, or produce downstream consequences faster than people can verify whether the underlying evidence remains valid.

Core risk

A system can execute correctly at the technical level and still misexecute if evidence, continuity, authority, scope, binding basis, commit decision, execution control, or outcome accountability is invalid or incomplete.

What TA-14 is

TA-14 treats execution as the visible end of a deeper evidentiary chain. Before a system acts, something was treated as real. That reality was recorded. The record was preserved or transformed. Continuity was maintained or degraded. Admissibility was determined or assumed. Reliance became binding. Binding moved toward commit. Commit authorized execution. Execution produced an outcome.

TA-14 governs that full progression.

The TA-14 admissible-execution chain

Reality → Record → Continuity → Admissibility → Binding → Commit → Execution → Outcome

1
RealityThe real-world condition, event, state, environment, obligation, or operational fact at issue.
2
RecordThe captured evidence, observation, signal, log, source document, or data object representing reality.
3
ContinuityThe preserved connection between reality and record across time, transformation, interpretation, and use.
4
AdmissibilityThe threshold determination that evidence is sufficient, current, authoritative, scoped, and reliable.
5
BindingThe point where evidence becomes reliance or begins to constrain downstream action.
6
CommitThe controlled boundary where a person, system, workflow, or institution commits to a consequence-bearing path.
7
ExecutionThe action, intervention, decision, routing, release, refusal, escalation, or operational movement.
8
OutcomeThe resulting condition, consequence, record, verification state, accountability object, or new reality.

Architecture layers

Parent chain
The governing spine: Reality, Record, Continuity, Admissibility, Binding, Commit, Execution, Outcome.
Evidence Governance
Governs whether information becomes evidence and whether evidence remains healthy enough to support admissibility.
Reliance Governance
Governs what must be true before a system, person, institution, or downstream process may rely on evidence.
Authority Governance
Separates technical ability from admissible authority.
Binding and Commit
Identifies when information becomes reliance, reliance becomes obligation, obligation becomes commit, and commit becomes execution.
Outcome Accountability
Preserves the result of execution and records the new reality produced by action.

TA-14 and misexecution

Technical failure

The system malfunctioned, crashed, violated a rule, failed a test, or did not perform as designed.

Misexecution

The system acted or created consequence when the evidentiary and authority chain was not admissible.

The TA-14 question is not only “did the system work?” It is “should the system have been allowed to act?”

Evidence Governance inside TA-14

A data point, model output, sensor reading, document, log, or human statement does not automatically become admissible evidence because it exists. Evidence must be classified, preserved, connected, tested, scoped, and evaluated before it can support reliance.

Evidence continuity

Can evidence still be traced back to the real condition, source, or event it claims to represent?

Evidence health

Is the evidence complete, current, coherent, uncorrupted, and suitable for the consequence involved?

Evidence capacity

Is the evidence strong enough to support the level of reliance, binding, commit, or execution proposed?

What TA-14 is not

Not ordinary monitoring

Monitoring observes activity. TA-14 governs admissibility before consequence-bearing execution occurs.

Not audit logging

Logs may show what happened. TA-14 asks whether what happened should have been allowed.

Not a policy dashboard

Dashboards display controls. TA-14 governs the chain through which evidence becomes action and outcome.

Not only an execution gate

TA-14 includes execution-boundary governance, but begins at reality and continues through outcome.
