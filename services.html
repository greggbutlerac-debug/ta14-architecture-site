Core service question

Should this system be allowed to bind, commit, execute, or create reliance based on the evidence available right now?

Who TA-14 services are for

TA-14 is designed for settings where ordinary monitoring, documentation, policy language, or after-the-fact audit trails are not enough. If a system can create reliance or consequence, TA-14 asks whether the chain behind that action is admissible before execution occurs.

AI workflow owners

Teams deploying AI outputs, autonomous agents, model-assisted decisions, AI governance workflows, or operational recommendations that may influence action, reliance, or institutional decisions.

Environmental and HVACDR systems

Teams working with IAQ, refrigeration, atmospheric evidence, diagnostics, sensor records, intervention thresholds, post-intervention proof, or environmental integrity claims.

Adjacent governance builders

Institutional decision pathways

Partner review candidates

Production-readiness teams

Primary review offers

TA-14 review offers are structured to move from public-material clarity to deeper evidence review and production-readiness assessment. Each offer preserves a clear boundary: TA-14 provides admissible-execution review, not free architecture-building for a third-party system outside a defined engagement.

Offer 01

Public-Material Architecture Review

Best first step for public systems, partner candidates, and adjacent architectures.

A public-material review maps a system’s externally visible materials against the TA-14 chain: Reality → Record → Continuity → Admissibility → Binding → Commit → Execution → Outcome.

This is the right entry point when a system has public documentation, a website, GitHub repository, white paper, paper, demo, article, product page, or public claim set that can be reviewed without private access.

Good fit when

  • You want an independent architecture-facing read of a public system.
  • You need to identify strengths and gaps without accusing the system of failure.
  • You want to understand whether public materials show admissible-execution alignment.
  • You are evaluating a potential partner architecture or adjacent governance system.

Typical deliverables

Public-material evidence basis A clear list of reviewed materials and the limits of public evidence.
TA-14 chain mapping A structured mapping of observable strengths and gaps across the full chain.
Architecture posture finding A neutral classification such as strong, partial, emerging, commit-adjacent, or production-readiness path.
Next evidence package A concise list of what evidence would be needed for deeper review.
Offer 02

Evidence Gap Review

Best for teams that need to know whether their evidence can support reliance.

An Evidence Gap Review evaluates whether records, logs, sensor data, model outputs, source documents, baselines, thresholds, determinations, or post-action records are strong enough to support consequence-bearing reliance.

This review focuses on the upstream evidence layer: whether information has become governed evidence, whether that evidence remains continuous, and whether it can support admissibility.

Good fit when

  • Decisions depend on logs, data, records, sensor readings, or AI outputs.
  • There is uncertainty about whether evidence is current, complete, or authoritative.
  • Intervention or action may occur before evidence is strong enough.
  • You need to separate ordinary information from admissible evidence.

Typical deliverables

Evidence inventory A structured view of the records, signals, documents, outputs, observations, or determinations available.
Continuity and chronology review Identification of continuity gaps, stale evidence, missing baselines, missing thresholds, or weak traceability.
Reliance-risk finding A finding on whether evidence can support the level of reliance being proposed.
Evidence strengthening path A defined set of evidence improvements required before stronger admissibility claims can be made.
Offer 03

Production-Readiness Review

Best for consequence-bearing workflows approaching deployment, scaling, or institutional use.

A Production-Readiness Review evaluates whether a system is governed across every consequence-bearing route. The central question is whether anything consequential can happen outside the admissibility chain.

This is the deeper review for systems moving beyond concept, prototype, public claim, or internal experiment toward real operational reliance.

Good fit when

  • A system is moving toward deployment, scaling, pilots, certification-readiness, or institutional adoption.
  • You need route-complete evidence from live reality through outcome.
  • There are binding, commit, execution, refusal, or escalation boundaries to test.
  • You need to know whether inadmissible action fails closed before consequence attaches.

Typical deliverables

Route-complete chain review Evaluation of consequence-bearing routes across Reality, Record, Continuity, Admissibility, Binding, Commit, Execution, and Outcome.
Boundary and refusal review Review of binding points, commit boundaries, escalation routes, refusal conditions, and fail-closed behavior.
Production-readiness classification A finding on whether the system is not ready, partially ready, controlled-pass ready, or ready for deeper operational validation.
Minimum evidence package The evidence required to support stronger readiness, partner, or certification-adjacent claims.

How a TA-14 review works

TA-14 reviews are structured, evidence-first, and boundary-controlled. The process is designed to avoid vague architecture talk and move quickly toward evidence, scope, findings, and next-step clarity.

Initial intake

The requesting party identifies the system, consequence-bearing action, reliance pathway, evidence basis, authority basis, binding or commit point, and what happens if the system is wrong.

Scope classification

TA-14 classifies the request as public-material review, evidence gap review, environmental integrity review, partner alignment review, production-readiness review, or not currently reviewable.

Evidence basis review

The available materials are reviewed according to the agreed scope. Public-material reviews stay within public evidence. Operational reviews require submitted evidence objects, logs, examples, records, or workflow proof.

TA-14 chain mapping

The reviewed system is mapped across Reality, Record, Continuity, Admissibility, Binding, Commit, Execution, and Outcome, with strengths and gaps clearly distinguished.

Findings and classification

The review produces a neutral finding, review classification, admissibility gaps, evidence requirements, and recommended next scope where appropriate.

What you receive

Review report

Chain map
A clear view of where the system has evidence across Reality, Record, Continuity, Admissibility, Binding, Commit, Execution, and Outcome.

What makes a good TA-14 review candidate?

Strong fit

  • The system creates or influences consequence-bearing action.
  • There is a clear evidence basis to review.
  • There is a real reliance pathway.
  • Authority, scope, or commit boundaries matter.
  • The team wants strengths-plus-gaps review, not marketing validation.
  • The requesting party is willing to define scope, evidence, and value exchange.

Weak fit

  • The request is only a general brainstorming conversation.
  • The system has no evidence materials to review.
  • The requester wants TA-14 language without TA-14 participation.
  • The goal is endorsement rather than admissible-execution review.
  • The request would require free architecture-building before scope.
  • The system cannot identify what consequence-bearing action is involved.

Review boundaries and non-claims

TA-14 services are deliberately bounded. This protects the requesting party, the reviewed system, and the TA-14 architecture from overclaiming.

TA-14 reviews do not automatically certify a system

A review may identify alignment, gaps, readiness path, or evidence requirements. Certification or approval language requires a separate written scope and sufficient evidence.

TA-14 reviews are not legal opinions

TA-14 may address evidentiary architecture and admissible-execution posture, but it does not replace legal, regulatory, security, or compliance counsel.

Public-material reviews are limited to public evidence

Public-material reviews do not claim private capabilities, hidden controls, undisclosed logs, or production behavior that was not made available for review.

TA-14 does not provide free build-before-boundary work

Roadmaps, implementation structures, test matrices, proof-object designs, partner language, and production readiness planning require a paid or clearly reciprocal engagement.
