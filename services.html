Review process

A simple process with serious evidentiary discipline.

Claims stay bounded

No build-before-boundary

Deep architecture shaping, implementation guidance, proof-object design, test matrices, maturity roadmaps, certification-readiness language, and partner enablement materials require a written scope, paid engagement, reciprocal evidence exchange, or approved partner-review framework.

Review boundaries

Clear boundaries protect the review.

TA-14 review findings are architecture and admissible-execution findings. They are not a substitute for legal advice, regulatory approval, cybersecurity testing, safety certification, engineering signoff, medical advice, financial advice, or production validation unless expressly stated in a separate written scope.

Not a legal opinion

TA-14 may identify evidence, authority, continuity, reliance, or consequence issues, but it does not provide legal conclusions.

Not a security audit

TA-14 may review governance, evidence, and execution readiness, but it is not a penetration test, code audit, or vulnerability assessment.

Not certification by default

Review, alignment, readiness, certification-readiness, certification, endorsement, and production validation are separate claim categories.

Safe review posture

TA-14 reviews use evidence-first, chronology-first, continuity-first, strengths-plus-gaps language. Serious adjacent systems are treated seriously. Public-material gaps are not framed as proof that private capability is absent.
