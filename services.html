The Criteria That Drifted RAVI SHANKAR NRK RAVI SHANKAR NRK COO · Head of Operations & Strategy · P&L Leader | Scaled SBU 2.5x to ₹108 Cr ($13M) in 12 months | Target Operating Model · Lean Six Sigma · Cross-Functional Leadership | ex-Commander, Indian Navy July 9, 2026 On why every seat can stay live, every policy current, every check green—and the governance has already failed This is the sixth piece in a series on governance runtime architecture. The first five established that authority needs a traceable source, that a seat holding authority can lapse, that the situation justifying a decision can shift before it is acted on, that standing must be re-proven at the moment consequence becomes irreversible, and that a delegation chain must carry proof of standing across every transfer. All five assumed, quietly, that the standard under which every seat reads every rule stays put. This piece removes that assumption. A Vignette Consider a regional bank with a concentration limit: no single counterparty may exceed fifteen percent of the lending book. The limit is encoded in policy v2.3. The risk officer, Officer Chen, holds a live seat with current mandate and accreditation. The situation — the counterparty's financials, the sector outlook, the macro stress scenario — supports an exception. The systems that will execute it are available. The criteria attestation, recorded at decision time, references risk appetite statement v1.4, reaffirmed by the Board Risk Committee within its review period. The exception is granted. The trade is queued for settlement. Between decision and settlement, nothing changes in anything the runtime watches. Officer Chen's seat remains live. Policy v2.3 is not superseded. The counterparty's financials are stable. The rails are operational. But during that gap, the Board Risk Committee restates the bank's risk appetite in narrative form — not a policy change, not a version bump, nothing that would trigger an alert. The concentration limit is still fifteen percent, but what counts as a justified exception has narrowed. The assumptions embedded in the original approval — that the sector exposure was acceptable, that the stress scenario was bounded — are no longer assumed. Cases that qualified near the old boundary are no longer inside the new one. At settlement, every check passes. The policy is current, the officer is live, the situation is stable, the systems are up, the attestation is within tolerance. The trade binds. The consequence forms. And the governance has already failed. This is not a failure of detection. Every signal the runtime monitors is green. This is a failure of calibration: the standard against which every signal is evaluated has shifted, and the shift is invisible to any predicate calibrated inside the drifted standard. The runtime keeps enforcing coherence against a standard that no longer holds. A New Category of Drift Machine learning already has a rich vocabulary of drift, so it is worth being precise about why this is not a familiar concern wearing new clothes: Article content Every row above the last one has a detector. Data and model drift are caught by model risk management; policy drift by version control; and governance drift by delegation chains and access review. Criteria drift has no detector by construction: every instrument that could catch it is calibrated inside the standard that drifted. That is what makes it a second-order problem — not another thing that fails, but drift in the standard used to decide whether anything has failed. How It Fails in Practice Five recurring signatures: Silent reparameterization: Risk appetite is restated without a policy version change; the rules read differently while every rule check still passes. Interpretive succession: A successor re-qualifies a decision against a reconstructed standard rather than an inherited one; authority and context checks pass while the reading standard has changed. Tacit assumption decay: Assumptions embedded in the original approval quietly cease to hold, without any observable shift large enough to trip a context check. Boundary migration: The line between qualified and unqualified moves gradually; cases approved near the old line are stranded outside the new one. Monitoring decay: The set of things being watched no longer covers the failure classes that matter; everything watched stays green while an unwatched class quietly exhausts viability. In every one of these, the audit trail is impeccable, and the outcome is wrong. Governing Criteria The governing criteria of a regime, at any moment, consist of four things: the organization's declared risk appetite, the interpretive framework under which rules are read onto cases, the tacit assumptions embedded in qualifications, and the decision boundary separating qualified from unqualified cases. The criteria are not the rule. They are the conditions under which the rule is read. A decision made at some moment carries the conclusion of a reading; without a record of the criteria in force at that moment, it does not carry the standard that shaped the reading. A successor re-qualifying without that record is reconstructing the standard, not inheriting it. The formal treatment—the criteria tuple, the drift function, and its tolerance schema—lives in the Criteria Validity—Technical Companion. The Fifth Field in the Chain of Custody The architectural response is simple to state. At decision time, the runtime records not only the policy version, the authority token, the context qualification, and the resource reservation but also a fifth field: an attestation of the governing criteria used to interpret them. If those criteria materially change before the commitment binds, the decision becomes structurally stale and must be re-qualified under the new standard. The attestation converts the governing criteria from an ambient assumption into a governed, versioned, auditable object. Its validity rests on three conditions: the attestation exists; the criteria have not drifted beyond a tolerance the criteria-owning seat declared; and that seat has re-affirmed the standard within a defined period—an active statement that the standard is still the standard, not merely an absence of change notices. This is checked periodically and whenever the criteria change, so the check at the moment of binding stays cheap. The same pattern extends one layer up. Whatever governs the validity of the system description itself—the preceding question of whether this is still the right description of the world to govern within—publishes versioned attestations too. The runtime never evaluates their content. It only guarantees that no decision binds under a standard, criteria, or description that it was not qualified against. This induces a strict dependency: criteria validity underwrites qualification validity, which underwrites binding validity. A qualification is only as valid as the criteria under which it was made; a binding is only as valid as the qualification it commits. Second-order drift breaks the chain at its first link—which is precisely why no amount of first-order rechecking can detect it. The Path Between Two Green Snapshots One subtlety deserves a paragraph even in a conceptual treatment. Two snapshots of a decision's standing can both look fully admissible while the trajectory between them has quietly spent its room to continue. The floor held throughout; every seat stayed live—and the standing room shrank anyway. That narrowing is a property of the path, not of either endpoint, so no present-state check, however green, can see it. Only a monitor built to read the path can. And the choice of what to monitor is itself a judgment—one that can drift in exactly the same second-order way. The monitor set, therefore, joins the governing criteria as a fifth element—the tuple extends from the four named above to five—and changing it is a criteria event, attested like any other. The Stack, in One View Article content The criteria layer sits above everything else because it is evaluated against everything below it—including the terms on which the basin itself was parameterized. Whether the system description remains valid is the still-higher question, consumed as an attestation, never computed within the runtime. The Result A determination is not a single event, not even a chain of seats. It is a lifecycle governed at every stage by criteria whose own validity must be attested, versioned, and re-proven before consequence forms. The historical signature is genuine but insufficient. The present standing at commitment is what matters. And the criteria under which both were evaluated must themselves remain valid across the interval—or every predicate passes while the governance has already failed. The architecture does not merely authorize action. It authorizes the commitment of action: re-proving the base invariants at the moment commitment becomes irreversible, within the structural floor the basin provides, against criteria whose own validity is attested, with evidence, with escalation, and with a seat that can still answer. The chain of custody does not end at the signature. It ends where someone can still answer for every link, at every gate, under a standard that is still the standard when the consequence becomes irreversible. — Commander Ravi Shankar NRK (Retd.), AI Strategy, Governance & Risk The formal specification—the criteria tuple, the drift function and tolerance schema, the attestation record format, the standing states, and worked algorithms and failure semantics—lives in the Criteria Validity—Technical Companion.
