Why this network matters

Serious adjacent systems should not be treated as competitors, clones, or footnotes.

The Partner Review Network gives serious governance builders a dignified path into the TA-14 ecosystem without erasing their work. It creates a written structure where independent systems can be recognized for what they do well while TA-14 preserves its role as the parent admissible-execution review architecture.

This is the difference between extraction and alignment. No one receives unlimited TA-14 architecture value for free. No partner receives uncontrolled TA-14 status language. But serious systems can still be reviewed, respected, positioned, and routed into client value through a defined pathway.

Partners keep their systems, names, and architectural identities.

Network model

TA-14 is the second-layer review authority, not a free architecture builder.

The Partner Review Network is designed for independent systems that may contribute specialized evidence, runtime, trust, policy, refusal, audit, environmental, or governance capabilities. TA-14 does not merge with those systems or certify them by association. It reviews defined routes, evidence claims, and consequence-bearing pathways under written scope.

01

Partner keeps identity

The partner system remains its own architecture. TA-14 does not absorb it, rename it, or imply that every serious governance system must become TA-14.

02

TA-14 reviews the route

TA-14 maps the partner’s consequence-bearing route against the admissible-execution chain to determine where evidence, authority, continuity, binding, commit, execution, and outcome are proven, partial, missing, or not reviewable.

03

Written boundaries control use

TA-14 name use, review authority, alignment language, certification-readiness language, public claims, economics, and client use must be approved in writing.

Review lanes

Partner pathways are scoped by what the partner actually proves.

TA-14 partner review lanes remain narrow, written, and evidence-based. The goal is to preserve independence while making it clear where TA-14 review begins, what it covers, and what it does not cover.

E

Evidence maturity

For partner systems that organize, preserve, validate, or evaluate evidence before reliance.

R

Runtime governance

For systems that route, constrain, refuse, escalate, or govern execution under runtime pressure.

P

Policy and trust layers

For systems that validate identity, standing, authorization, policy, or trust claims before action.

A

Admissible-execution review

TA-14 performs the second-layer review of the full consequence-bearing route.

Controlled progression

The network matures through written scope, evidence, review, and approved language.

Partner-review status should be earned through evidence and preserved through clear boundaries. The network is strongest when each entity can show what it contributes while TA-14 preserves the review layer that determines whether consequence-bearing execution is admissible.

1. Identify the partner lane Determine whether the entity belongs in runtime governance, evidence maturity, policy/trust, governance evidence, environmental integrity, or another bounded review lane.
2. Preserve independent identity Define what the partner architecture is, what it is not, and what claims it may make without implying merger, endorsement, or TA-14 certification.
3. Map the route to TA-14 Review the submitted route against Reality, Record, Continuity, Admissibility, Binding, Commit, Execution, and Outcome.
4. Approve public language Any TA-14-reviewed, TA-14-aligned, TA-14-backed, certification-ready, route-complete, or admissible-execution language must be approved in writing.
5. Route client value cleanly When TA-14 creates client-facing value through review authority, language, credibility, or second-layer admissible-execution review, TA-14 participates under written terms.

Non-claims

Partner review is not merger, endorsement, blanket certification, or unrestricted name use.

The Partner Review Network is intentionally controlled. It protects TA-14 from open-ended architecture extraction while giving serious adjacent builders a clean route for bounded cooperation, review, and client-facing value.

Not absorption

TA-14 does not erase the partner’s architecture or claim ownership of the partner’s system. A strong partner remains strong because its own role is preserved clearly.

Not endorsement

A review pathway is not a blanket endorsement of the company, product, deployment, security posture, legal posture, or unreviewed implementation.

Not free implementation design

Deep architecture shaping, test matrices, evidence packet design, maturity roadmaps, or client-use language require written scope, payment, partnership, or reciprocal value.
