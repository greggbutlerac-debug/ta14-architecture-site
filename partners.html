TA-14 Partner Review Network

Independent architectures. Written boundaries. Second-layer admissible-execution review.

The TA-14 Partner Review Network supports serious adjacent governance builders without absorbing their systems, erasing their identity, or allowing unbounded use of TA-14 language. Partners keep their own architecture. TA-14 provides the second-layer admissible-execution review across Reality, Record, Continuity, Admissibility, Binding, Commit, Execution, and Outcome.

Network model

TA-14 is the second-layer review authority, not a free architecture builder.

The Partner Review Network is designed for independent systems that may contribute specialized evidence, runtime, trust, policy, refusal, audit, environmental, or governance capabilities. TA-14 does not merge with those systems or certify them by association. It reviews defined routes, evidence claims, and consequence-bearing pathways under written scope.

01

Partner keeps identity

The partner system remains its own architecture. TA-14 does not absorb it, rename it, or imply that every serious governance system must become TA-14.

02

TA-14 reviews the route

TA-14 maps the partner’s consequence-bearing route against the admissible-execution chain to determine where evidence, authority, continuity, binding, commit, execution, and outcome are proven, partial, missing, or not reviewable.

03

Written boundaries control use

TA-14 name use, review authority, alignment language, certification-readiness language, public claims, economics, and client use must be approved in writing.

Core boundary

No partner may privately deliver the TA-14 layer.

A partner may provide its own specialized assessment. TA-14 performs the second-layer admissible-execution review. No partner may self-certify as TA-14-reviewed, TA-14-aligned, TA-14-backed, route-complete, certification-ready, or admissible-execution reviewed without separate written approval.

Review lanes

Partner pathways are scoped by what the partner actually proves.

TA-14 partner review lanes should remain narrow, written, and evidence-based. The goal is to preserve independence while making it clear where TA-14 review begins, what it covers, and what it does not cover.

E

Evidence maturity

For partner systems that organize, preserve, validate, or evaluate evidence before reliance.

R

Runtime governance

For systems that route, constrain, refuse, escalate, or govern execution under runtime pressure.

P

Policy and trust layers

For systems that validate identity, standing, authorization, policy, or trust claims before action.

A

Admissible-execution review

TA-14 performs the second-layer review of the full consequence-bearing route.

Non-claims

Partner review is not merger, endorsement, blanket certification, or unrestricted name use.

The Partner Review Network is intentionally controlled. It protects TA-14 from open-ended architecture extraction while giving serious adjacent builders a clean route for bounded cooperation, review, and client-facing value.

Not absorption

TA-14 does not erase the partner’s architecture or claim ownership of the partner’s system.

Not endorsement

A review pathway is not a blanket endorsement of the company, product, deployment, security posture, legal posture, or unreviewed implementation.

Not free implementation design

Deep architecture shaping, test matrices, evidence packet design, maturity roadmaps, or client-use language require written scope, payment, partnership, or reciprocal value.
