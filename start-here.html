Plain English

TA-14 asks whether the evidence behind action is admissible enough before the action creates consequence.

Reality → Record → Continuity → Admissibility → Binding → Commit → Execution → Outcome

Before action

What real condition is being represented? What evidence exists? Is it current, continuous, attributable, and strong enough for the proposed reliance?

At the boundary

Who has authority? Where does evidence become reliance? Where does reliance become binding? Where does the route enter commit?

After outcome

What actually happened? Was the result preserved? Can the decision, execution, and outcome be replayed, reviewed, defended, and inherited by the next route?

The core distinction

Capability asks whether a system can produce an output or take an action. Admissibility asks whether that output or action has earned the right to matter.

Live Gate and API

TA-14 is not only doctrine. It has a public decision surface.

The TA-14 Gate is the control point where a proposed consequence-bearing route is evaluated before it is allowed to bind, commit, execute, or create reliance. The public API exposes six evaluation routes and returns ALLOW, HOLD, DENY, or ESCALATE classifications.

Evaluate execution

Classifies a proposed action route across evidence, continuity, authority, binding, commit, and outcome conditions.

Evaluate evidence

Tests whether evidence is sufficient, current, continuous, attributable, and fit for governed reliance.

Check authority

Tests identity, delegated authority, scope, legitimacy, and subject-action alignment.

Validate continuity

Tests sequence, lineage, custody, timing, and material continuity across the route.

Create reviewability record

Produces a structured record of submitted conditions, failed links, warnings, and next steps.

Screen AI procurement

Tests whether a product, vendor, pilot, or deployment exposes unresolved admissibility gaps.

What the public sandbox proves

It proves that TA-14 can operate as a developer-facing, machine-readable decision surface with deterministic decision classes, typed requests, structured responses, OpenAPI documentation, and bounded public logic.

What the public sandbox does not prove

The sandbox evaluates conditions submitted by the caller. It does not claim that every submitted assertion has already been independently authenticated against an external source. Evidence-connected production use may bind decisions to source records, identity, delegated authority, hashes, ruleset versions, signed receipts, persistent audit storage, re-evaluation, and outcome records.

What TA-14 is not

TA-14 is not merely monitoring, logging, compliance, authorization, or a gate.

TA-14 may use records, APIs, logs, dashboards, policies, gates, refusals, and reviews, but it is not reducible to any one of them. It governs the dependency chain behind consequence-bearing execution.

Monitoring
Shows conditions, activity, alerts, or system behavior.
TA-14 asks: are the conditions and records admissible enough for reliance and action?
Authorization
Determines who may access or invoke a resource.
TA-14 asks: is this specific action legitimate, in scope, evidence-supported, and admissible now?
Execution gate
Allows, blocks, holds, or escalates an action.
TA-14 asks: is the chain behind that gate decision itself admissible?
Audit trail
Records what happened after the fact.
TA-14 asks: did admissible evidence exist before reliance, binding, commit, and execution?

The clean distinction

Controls can stop execution. TA-14 governs whether allowing, holding, denying, or escalating was itself admissible.
